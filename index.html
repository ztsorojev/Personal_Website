About Me

When I was 10, I imagined that a little man was running on a wheel inside my computer in order to make it work. One day, I opened the case and discovered something else. I guess this is what sparked my interest in technology.

11 years later, I am an entrepreneur and a master student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at UCLA. My dream is to build a tool to change the way we learn new things. I am passionate about how the brain works and how we get new ideas. This is why, in Sept. 2016, I started the Outside the Box movement. Every week, our team meets to brainstorm on problems affecting society. I have created 2 chapters: one in Belgium and one in the US. Our long term vision is to spread on every campus throughout the world to have a global impact.

After thinking about new ideas, I also love making them come to life through design. Drawing has been a hobby of mine since I was 7 years old. Later, when I was 14, I fell in love with Photoshop and discovered a new way to express my ideas.

My plans for the future are to continue learning the skills I need to make my ideas come to life.
